When I'm inside my mind feels small and my body feels big. My mind feels small and my emotions feel big. My hand on my face is a gesture too big for the screen. When I roll over in my bed the neighbors can hear the bed creak, can feel the impact.

When I'm outside you cannot tell if its tears or mist or rain. You cannot tell the difference between a scream and the silence.

When I die in my bed it is a mess. There is blood everywhere and everyone knows. Everyone sees. No one forgets. My body is preserved and lives forever without me.

When I die outside, I disappear. No one hears, no one sees, and no one knows. I am forgotten immediately. My body already become something else.

Performance by Priscilla Gomez Cabrales
Score by Chris Norcross
Shot, Edited and Directed by Ali Lotz
